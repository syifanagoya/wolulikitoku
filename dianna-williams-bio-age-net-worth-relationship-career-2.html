Dianna Williams| Bio, Age, Net Worth, Relationship, Career

April 2024 · 5 minute read

American dance choreographer, businesswoman, and media personality Dianna Williams is well-known for her work on the dance reality TV series “Bring It!”

The primary focus of the performance was the competition between her dancing dolls and other dance groups. The show aired on Lifetime Channel from 2014 until 2018.

Ms D is her stage name. 2010 saw her open the “Dollhouse Dancing Factory” in Jackson, Mississippi, which would house her dancing troupe, the Dancing Dolls.

She also won the Best Doll Studio Award for her Dollhouse Studio. Moreover, she is active on social media, with 116k Twitter followers under the handle @I AM DIANNA and 1.4 million Instagram followers under the name @mrs d2u.

Where was Dianna Williams born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Dianna Williams was born in Jackson, Mississippi, on December 1, 1978, in the United States. Dianna Maines was her birth name. She holds American citizenship.

She is of African-American origin and a Sagittarius by sign. Wayne Maines, the father, and Kelly Maines, the mother, gave birth to her and her twin brother, Zurich Maines.

Her three sisters are Asher, Terra Angel, and Monique Carr. She was raised in Jackson, the city of her birth, together with her siblings. Her parents made her enroll in the Angie Luke School of Dance when she was four years old.

During the courses, she learned about a variety of dance forms, such as “Jazz,” “Modern,” “Ballet Point,” and “Belly Dance.” Dianna kept competing, taking first place in multiple dancing contests.

She received her education at Raymond High School. She also went on to enroll at Jackson State University, where she graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Criminal Justice in May 2005.

At California State Dominguez Hills University, she captained the cheerleading squad from 1989 to 1998 and took part in the “Eye of the Tiger Drill Team.” She was also a Recreation Aide and Dance Instructor for its many programs on the north side.

How did Dianna Williams start his Professional Career?

To begin her career as a dance choreographer, Dianna Williams opened the Dancing Dolls Dance Studio at the Pied Piper Playhouse in Jackson, Mississippi, in 2001.

She went on to form the Grove Park Dancerettes in 2002. She also choreographed the Callaway High School Chargettes from 2006 to 2008.

From 2004 to 2009, she was involved in the Jackson State University Twirlers’ costume design. She was a member of the modeling team at JSU.

A breakthrough in Dianna’s career occurred after she opened her dance studio, “Dancing Dolls Factory,” in Jackson, Mississippi, on August 23, 2010.

The Dancing Dolls Factory formed a dancing squad known as the Dancing Dolls team, which was captained by Miss D (Dianna). The squad is thriving and has won 15 Grand Champion titles in addition to over 100 honors.

Their slogan is “Dancing Dolls for Life.” Because of the great success of the store, she has built Dancing Dolls Factory locations in Atlanta, Georgia, and Birmingham, Alabama.

When she and her dancing dolls team began to appear on the American dance reality television series “Bring It!” in 2014, she became well-known.
