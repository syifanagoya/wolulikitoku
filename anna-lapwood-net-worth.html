Anna Lapwood Overview and Wiki

Early Beginnings and Education

Born in the serene town of High Wycombe, Anna Lapwood emerged into a family deeply rooted in caring and cultural enrichment. Her early exposure to music, guided by her parents’ professions, set the stage for a flourishing career.

Attending the Junior Royal Academy of Music was a pivotal chapter, where she not only honed her skills in multiple instruments but also embraced the complexities of the organ, an instrument that would later define her career.

Director of Music at Pembroke College, Cambridge

Appointed in 2016, Anna Lapwood became the Director of Music at Pembroke College, Cambridge, at just 21 years old. This role marked her as one of the youngest individuals to ever direct a choir at an Oxford or Cambridge University College.

Her leadership not only involves conducting the college’s chapel choir but also encompasses administrative and educational responsibilities, significantly influencing the college’s musical direction.

Founder of Pembroke College Girls’ Choir

In 2018, Lapwood founded the Pembroke College Girls’ Choir, which was designed to provide choral education to girls from local schools aged 11–18. This initiative not only expanded the college’s musical outreach but also offered young women opportunities for artistic development and performance at a high level.

Anna has been instrumental in initiating several innovative projects, including the Cambridge Organ Experience for Girls aimed at encouraging young females to take up organ playing. National and International Performances

As an accomplished organist, Lapwood has performed at some of the most iconic venues in the world, including the Royal Albert Hall and the Royal Festival Hall in London, and St Thomas Church in Manhattan, New York. Her performances are highly acclaimed, showcasing her versatility and depth as a musician.

Collaborations and Recordings

Her collaborative performances have also been noteworthy. In 2022, Anna performed alongside the electronic artist Bonobo during his residency at the Royal Albert Hall, an event that was both a musical and a personal highlight for her, widely recognized on social media platforms.
