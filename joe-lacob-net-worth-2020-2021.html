Early Life

Joseph Steven Lacob, commonly known as Joe Lacob, was born on January 10, 1956, in New Bedford, Massachusetts. His father, Sidney, worked at the paper product company, while his mother, Marlene, worked at night shifts at a local supermarket. Joe came from a very poor family and was the first person in his family to graduate with a degree.

Growing up, Joe was a fan of ‘Boston Celtics.’ The company, his father, use to work was shut down and shifted to another location. His father was the only person who was retained and transferred to the new location by the company. So the entire Lacob family moved to Anaheim, California.

As a kid, Joe was a sincere student and always performed better in studies. After finishing his schooling, for further studies, he went to the University of California, Irvine. There he took the subject of biological science and graduated with a Bachelor’s degree. After his bachelor’s, Joe moved to Los Angeles for his master’s degree. He did a master’s in public health from the University of California, Los Angeles, and an MBA from the Stanford School of Business.

Joe was once married to Laurie. But the couple divorced due to their personal differences. Together, they have two kids. After getting a divorce, Joe was single for quite a long time until he met Nicole Curran, a beautiful brunette, who is the board of directors of the Warriors Community Foundation. After dating for a long period, the couple is engaged now.

Career

Before joining Kleiner Perkins, he was at the executive positions at various other firms. He has been with Kleiner Perkins for almost 30 years. Lacob has always worked hard to achieve his dreams.

Joe Lacob was also one of the primary investors in the ‘American Basketball League,’ which is a women’s professional basketball league. The time he invested in the league, it was a total failure. A childhood ‘Celtics’ fan, in 2006, Joe became the part-owner of the ‘Boston Celtics,’ a team from the ‘National Basketball Association’ (NBA). It was a dream come true for him, even though after moving to Los Angeles, he had started supporting ‘Los Angeles Lakers.’

After four years as a part-owner of ‘Celtics,’ a big opportunity came knocking on his door. Joe Lacob, along with his group of investors, won out over a dozen of other bids for the ‘Golden State Warriors’, an NBA team. They closed the deal for $450 million. Having already a much-needed experience of handling an NBA team, he started doing good business at the ‘Warriors.’

After taking over ‘Warriors,’ he made some tough decisions at the management level and made some major changes in the club. Under him, ‘Golden State Warriors’ have won two NBA titles.
